## Case study 1 — Shake Shack Japan: official website renewal

**Client**: Shake Shack Japan（サイトのWorks/Magazineに掲載済みの公開実績）
**Services**: UI design, front-end development

### Challenge

Shake Shack, the New York-born burger brand, needed its Japanese official site renewed without losing the brand's premium feel — while significantly improving usability for a mobile-first Japanese audience.

### What we did

- Redesigned the menu pages and improved the store locator; added a news section
- Mobile-first responsive design across all pages
- Deliberately framework-free implementation (HTML / CSS / JavaScript) for a lightweight, fast site — we handled the project end to end, from UI design to front-end build

### Outcome

- Page load speed improved roughly **40% compared to the pre-renewal site**
- Achieved green scores on **all Core Web Vitals metrics**

## Case study 2 — Regional farm: website built on the equal exchange model

**Client**: A regional farm in Japan（相手が特定されない範囲で記述。公開可否は要確認）
**Services**: Web design & production, ongoing improvement

### Challenge

A family farm had a story worth telling — genuine produce, a face-to-face way of doing business — but no marketing budget. In regional Japan this is common: decades of craft, zero communications spend.

### What we did

- Proposed our **equal exchange compensation** model: the farm pays for its website with a monthly box of fresh vegetables instead of money
- Designed and built the site, then stayed on for continuous improvement after launch — the exchange structure turns a one-off project into an ongoing partnership

### Outcome

- The farm gained its first owned media presence without cash outlay
- The relationship shifted from client-vendor to partners producing value together; we continue iterating on the site months after launch
- The model itself became a talking point that draws attention to the farm's story（定性的成果。定量数値は [[要確認:数値]]）
